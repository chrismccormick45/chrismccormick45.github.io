Experience

I joined my school's Mars Rover Team to further my understanding of physics and engineering. In this project, our team designed a rover to perform a series of tasks such as analyzing dirt samples for life and autonomous retrieval missions. My subgroup applied physics principles to design the chassis, drivetrain, and arm of our rover. Joining this team has allowed me to work collaboratively with other students to solve engineering challenges. Every Sunday during the school year, the whole team would meet, integrate all the subgroups parts, and would focus on working on and assembling the rover, and fixing any design flaws that would arise. In June, our rover was placed in a competition with rovers designed by students from some of the most prestigious universities in the world.

I have also taken every opportunity to apply physics principles to the outside world. As such, I designed and built a capacitor after taking a class in electricity and magnetism. At its simplest form, a capacitor is just two conductors separated by an insulator, so I used a plastic jar filled with salt water and wrapped in foil. After some trial and error, I found it was easiest to charge the capacitor by rubbing a piece of fabric against a PVC pipe, then moving the statically charged pipe close to the salt water.
