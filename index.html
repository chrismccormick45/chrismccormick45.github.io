Experience

Design, Build, Fly at UCLA

This last year, I joined the Manufacturing team of Design/Build/Fly (DBF) at UCLA. In this club, students are challenged to design a plane capable of completing a series of missions laid out by the intercollegiate AIAA Design/Build/Fly Competition. Though I initially joined the Aerodynamics team, and found designing the the nose, stabilizers, and wings of the airplane using CFD, MATLAB, and SolidWorks very interesting, I ultimately found myself on the Manufacturing team which was much more hands-on and newcomer friendly. While on this team, I helped contribute to the overall design of the airplane and helped assemble the stabilizers. After competing in the annual AIAA Design/Build/Fly Competition, our team placed 15th out of 81 competing teams (the highest placement ever achieved by UCLA)!

Engineering Course: E96A Plane Design

During the 2023 Winter Quarter at UCLA, I enrolled in a voluntary, hands-on airplane engineering course. In this class, students were broken into teams to design, test, and assemble a remote controlled airplane. I assumed the lead role on my team, and helped divide up tasks among the team members.

After settling on a general plane design, my team ran instructor-provided MATLAB code to determine the general weight, size, and wingspan of our airplane. We rigorously selected the airfoils for our wing and stabilizers using these attributes, the coefficients of lift and drag of different airfoils, and the goals we wanted to achieve. After creating and assembling our entire airplane in SolidWorks, we continuously added, subtracted, and redesigned different components throughout the quarter. This was because, after assembling and testing different parts of our airplane, we found certain designs inferior to others. When we were finished, it was incredibly rewarding to see our hard work pay off when our airplane flew and set new records.

In addition to making history by successfully designing the first twin-prop airplane ever to built in the E96A Planes course, my team's plane won two of the five available awards after competing in our class competition. We won the awards for the fastest and the most stable plane.

Engineering Course: E96R Rocket Engineering

During the 2022 Fall Quarter at UCLA, I enrolled in a voluntary, hands-on rocket engineering course. In this course, students formed teams and were tasked with designing, testing, and assembling two solid motor rockets. The first rocket was used a C-class rocket motor and the second rocket used a G-class rocket motor.

After testing different designs on a class-provided model rocket program called OpenRocket, my team settled on a relatively mainstream design for our first rocket. We then created each component and assembled the rocket in Solidworks prior to 3D printing. It took a couple reprints and redesign choices before we had a rocket we were satisfied with. However, when we launched, our rocket did not achieve anywhere near the apogee or speed that had hoped and desgined for.

When it came time to design the second rocket, my team was determined to do better. Each team member conducted their own independent research and suggested different ideas to the group. In creating this new rocket, we incorporated parts of the first rocket that we thought worked well, but also redesigned other components and added new components. This was due to the components not working as intended on the first rocket and due to the higher apogee and speeds expected from this new G-class motor. In addition to all these changes, we also experimented with different materials. In the end, our seconed rocket exceeded our expectations on launch by achieving an apogee of about 3200 ft. Saddleback Robotics

At my previous institution, I joined the robotics club's Mars Rover Team. This team was tasked with the ground-up design and assembly of a radio-controller rover that was capable of navigating rough terrain, driving autonomously, picking up and dragging heavy objects with its mechanical arm, and analyzing dirt samples for signs of life. Though I also helped different subteams with assembly, I decided to join the chassis and electrical subteams. On these subteams, I was assigned to design, 3D model, and assemble different components within the rover's chassis. These components mainly consisted of electrical circuits and their corresponding housing boxes, however I also worked on assembling different components that connected to the rover's drivetrain.
